Induction vs Recursion

Induction is bottom-up.


Recursion is top-down.


In parsing, YACC is bottom-up, PEG is top-down.


YACC is LR(k), PEG is recursive-descent.


YACC is context-free, PEG can depend on context.


LR(k) is theoretical, PEG is practical.


Semi-colons exist in PL[1]s because of YACC.  PEG doesn't require semi-colons in PLs.


Possible rewording: top-down is operational, whereas bottom-up is declarative[2].


Top-down implies the use of a Stack[3].  Bottom-up does not.


[Use of a Stack defies parallelism.]


"Context free" means that time[4] has been removed, there is no f(t).[5]

[1] PL means Programming Language.

[2] Question to self: Is this a correct characterization?

[3] A stack of breadcrumbs - to "remember" where you were.

[4] Time is also known as History.  History is also known as State.

[5] Lack of f(t) has been decried by Ilya Prigogene and Grace Hopper.
